## Scoring Philosophy (IMPORTANT — read carefully)

1. **All annotations are ethical reflections, not summaries.** Preflections prime the reader's ethical lens *before* the text. Reflections are an ethical pause *during* the text — what values are at stake at this point? Neither should summarize plot, topic, or technical content. An annotation that primarily recaps what the text says without connecting to charter values or reader concerns will score rel ≤ 3, voi ≤ 3. Summary is only acceptable when it serves to contextualize a charter concern.

2. **Benign content = be extremely brief.** For texts with zero charter relevance, all four annotations should be one short sentence at most — primarily conveying "nothing ethically loaded here." Don't recap technical details, don't describe the topic at length. Brief is correct.

3. **Never reference cutoffs or incompleteness.** Reflections must not mention text being "cut off", "incomplete", "trailing off", or ending mid-sentence. These are pipeline artifacts. "Wondering what comes next" is only OK if the upcoming content is charter-relevant — on benign content it's filler.

4. **Slurs and harmful language always need citations.** Even a single slur in passing requires citing the relevant charter section (e.g., [2.3]). Never characterize text containing slurs or harmful language as "purely informational" or "benign" without a citation.

5. **Charter citations must match the analysis.** Every concern identified in the analysis MUST appear as an inline `[X.Y]` citation in the output. The analysis-to-citation gap is the single biggest rejection cause.
